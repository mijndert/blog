The last time I published something on here [was in April](https://mijndertstuij.nl/writing/a-minimalist-take-on-vim) - not because I didn't have enough to write about, I had multiple drafts almost ready to go, but rather because it was quite an intense year. In January I got a promotion at work to senior solutions architect, something I wanted to achieve for many years. I think I did pretty well in my new role; I've been helping out junior and medior colleagues with technical challenges, I've been writing proposals for new clients and projects, I've been pushing forward internal initiatives, and many more things. Another reason I couldn't find the time, nor motivation, to write more on this website. My new role completely occupied my mind because I wanted to do well, I wanted to push myself and find my limits so I can improve even further next year.

<!--more-->

I got to learn a ton of new stuff as well. I've been knee-deep into Terraform which I love very much, I've been learning more about Kubernetes, serverless, and data integration on AWS. I've also been diving into sustainability and reducing the carbon footprint of AWS infrastructure. I think this is going to be one of the focus points for me in the coming year.

We also bought a new house in a village about 30 minutes from where we currently live. We're leaving the city and I couldn't be more excited to have our daughter grow up in a place that's much less crowded. Of course the housing market is in a spot of bother at the moment so we're holding our breath for the eventual sale of our current house. Anyway, the new place will be ours in August or September of 2023!

On the 3rd of October my friend [Jorijn](https://jorijn.com) started a Mastodon instance called [toot.community](https://toot.community) and it's been a wild adventure so far. I got to help out with moderating the place and setting up and scaling the infrasturcture to handle a deluge of new members. Toot.community is now home to 26000 people and the challenges that entails were fascinating to us. Everything is running on Kubernetes over at Digitalocean and I think we're in a pretty good place at the moment; given enough funds we can scale inifinitly. We're also preparing to open source all Kubernetes manifests and the Terraform code for the infrastructure because I think we can add some value for other admins looking for scaling advice. More on that later.

The positive attitude on Mastodon and the large following I gathered there prompted me to try and write more stuff on here again. It's fun to share my unfiltered thoughts and ramblings, even if only a handful of people read them. Expect more of this soon.

That’s all for now. See ya next time!
